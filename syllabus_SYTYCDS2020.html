Example Syllabus for 10 week course

This is a syllabus for the 10 week course we taught at the University of Washington. Each week included two 80 minute periods. We rarely lectured for more than 15 at a time and incorporated many group activities.

Students were required to have an introductory level statistics course, but we motivated and taught testing and estimation in a way that did not require previous experience with these concepts.

The course drew students from a range of disciplines including statistics, economics, fisheries, communications, social work,… While the bulk of the students were upper level undergraduates, we always had a few graduate students. This breadth of experience and expertise contributed to the course, and led to many intesting group discussions.

Week Theme Description
1 Brains We make lots of cognitive mistakes.
2 Coincidence and patterns One is attaching meaning to patterns generated by chance.
3 Testing Testing is a logical approach to helping us with this problem
4 Problems with Testing However, here are many of problems with how tests have and continue to be used
5 Power and Projects Low powered tests create many of these problems.
6 Estimation and confidence intervals Estimation with confidence intervals makes it easier to identify and avoid many of these problems
7 What is a project Some examples that illustrate the complexities of statistics in science
8 Communication Communication is probably the most important and difficult part of the process.
9 Life cycle of science Statistics cannot be disentangled from the scientific process. (question/metric , population/sampling, manuscript review, pre-registration,…)
10 Project presentations Students present their projects
